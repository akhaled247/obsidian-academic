# Research Ethics

## Grant Process
1. Perform background research and literature review on the topic and find the identifiable gap
2. First round submission to (NIH, NSF, DOD, etc.)
	1. Only ~15% of NIH applications are funded on the first submission
	2. *Since funding by outside organization, must be tracked and managed very closely*
3. Principal Investigator (PI) have received funds and start research
	1. **THE UNIVERSITY** takes ownership of the data the PI & his team collects because grants are awarded to colleges/universities *on behalf* of the PI
	2. However, PI is responsible for data collection, management, and sharing.
### Data Sharing and Management Plan
* What type of data is being collected (file type, physical, biological, etc.)?
* What is needed to analyze these data (software, documentation, tools, etc.)?
* Are there standardized/accepted ways of analyzing these data (based on research already in the field)?
* Where? When? How long? -- Especially in regards to patient-related data, since must comply with university-approved protocols (PII)
* How will you remove sensitive information and comply with ethics (PII, HIPAA-compliancy, etc.)?
* Who will manage the data and how (e.g. Dropbox, Google Drive, etc.)?
* **Open Science**
	* Open access, educational resources, data, source, peer review, methodology
	* For PI to determine, not us as students

>[!error] Students do NOT own any data they collect
><div style="color:#fb464c"><b><ul><li>Do not <i>take data</i> with you when they leave the lab without <i>explicit approval</i> from the PI</li><li>Do not <i>upload data</i> to personal drives or share externally without permission</li><li>Return <i>all data and materials</i> at the end of this experience</li></ul></b></div>

- Cannot make detailed early, published findings in your college essays
- Cannot upload **any** data without explicit permission from the PI
